# Project Constitution: Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics Handbook

## 1. Introduction

This document outlines the core principles, vision, and governance for the **Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics Handbook** project. It serves as a guiding star for all contributors and stakeholders, ensuring alignment and clarity in our collective endeavors.

- **Constitution Version:** 0.1.0

## 2. Vision

To create an accessible, hands-on handbook on Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics, empowering beginners and intermediate learners with practical knowledge and skills in both hardware and software aspects.

## 3. Core Principles

### 3.1. Hands-on Learning
The handbook MUST prioritize practical examples, projects, and exercises to facilitate experiential learning and skill development.

### 3.2. Beginner-Friendly Progression
Content MUST be structured to be accessible to beginners, gradually introducing complex concepts with clear explanations and supporting materials suitable for intermediate learners.

### 3.3. Comprehensive Coverage
The handbook MUST provide balanced and thorough documentation covering both the hardware and software components essential for Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics.

### 3.4. Clarity and Accuracy
All explanations, instructions, and documentation MUST be clear, concise, easy to understand, and technically accurate.

## 4. Success Criteria

The project will be deemed successful when it:
- Effectively engages and educates both beginner and intermediate learners in Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics.
- Equips readers with practical skills demonstrated through successful completion of hands-on projects.
- Provides clear, accurate, and comprehensive documentation for both hardware and software aspects.
- Receives positive feedback and high satisfaction ratings from the target audience.

## 5. Constraints

- **Target Audience:** Content MUST be tailored for beginners to intermediate learners.
- **Content Scope:** MUST cover both hardware and software aspects of Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics.
- **Learning Approach:** MUST emphasize hands-on learning, practical applications, and project-based instruction.

## 6. Stakeholders

- **Readers:** Beginners and intermediate learners interested in Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics.
- **Authors/Contributors:** Individuals responsible for creating and maintaining the handbook's content.
- **Editors/Publishers:** Responsible for editorial quality, production, and distribution.
- **Technical Reviewers:** Experts who ensure the technical accuracy and relevance of the content.

## 7. Brand Voice

The brand voice for the handbook is informative, encouraging, practical, clear, and engaging. It aims to demystify complex topics and inspire readers through a supportive and authoritative tone.

## 8. Governance

### 8.1. Amendment Procedure
This constitution can be amended by a **consensus of the core authoring team, with final approval from the lead editor**.

### 8.2. Versioning Policy
The constitution adheres to Semantic Versioning (MAJOR.MINOR.PATCH).
- **MAJOR** version increments for backward-incompatible changes (e.g., removal of a core principle).
- **MINOR** version increments for new principles or significant expansions.
- **PATCH** version increments for clarifications, wording fixes, or non-semantic refinements.

### 8.3. Compliance Review
Compliance with this constitution will be reviewed **annually** by **the core authoring and editorial team**.
